## Design Context

### Users
- **Owner**: Product designer aspiring to a Senior UX Designer role
- **Visitors**: Recruiters, UX project leaders, project managers
- **Goal**: Visitors should feel confident this person is senior-level, and be impressed by the consistency and creative quality — minimal yet current with 2026 UX design trends

### Brand Personality
- **Voice**: Minimalist, bold, sober
- **Tone**: Original but calm, as sober as possible
- **Emotional goal**: Trust + admiration for creative consistency

### Anti-references
- Must NOT look generic or corporate
- Must NOT feel like a template
- Must feel original but restrained

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light + dark mode with system detection (both must be polished)
- **Color**: Open to suggestion — current cyan/red is placeholder defaults
- **Typography**: Open to suggestion — Geist fonts are placeholder
- **Style**: Minimal, contemporary, confident. Aligned with 2026 UX trends.

### Accessibility
- WCAG AA compliance required

### Design Principles
1. **Confident restraint** — Every element earns its place. Bold through precision, not volume.
2. **Senior craft signals** — Typography, spacing, and color choices should telegraph design maturity.
3. **Quiet originality** — Distinct from templates without being loud. The details differentiate.
4. **Trend-aware, not trend-driven** — Reflect current design thinking without chasing fads.
5. **Recruiter-ready** — Scannable, professional, with clear hierarchy that respects busy reviewers' time.
